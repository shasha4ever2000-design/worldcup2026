The 2026 format: 48 teams, 104 matches

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the first to feature 48 teams (up from 32) and the first hosted by three countries — the United States, Canada and Mexico. It runs from June 11 to July 19, 2026 and packs in a record 104 matches.

The 48 teams are drawn into 12 groups of four. Each team plays the other three in its group once. The top two of every group, plus the eight best third-placed teams, advance — making 32 teams in the knockout stage. From there it's straight elimination: Round of 32 → Round of 16 → quarter-finals → semi-finals → final.

How the knockouts work

With 12 groups, the eight best third-placed teams join the 24 automatic qualifiers (group winners and runners-up) to form a 32-team Round of 32 — a stage unique to the expanded format.

  • Round of 32 — 16 matches, losers go home.
  • Round of 16 — the last 16.
  • Quarter-finals — the last 8.
  • Semi-finals — the last 4.
  • Third-place play-off & Final — July 18 & 19.
